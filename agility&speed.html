Agility & Speed Exercises

A comprehensive guide to improving agility and speed for all fitness levels.

For Beginners

Exercise Description
1. Cone Weave Place 5-6 cones in a straight line. Weave in and out of the cones, focusing on quick, controlled steps.
2. Ladder Drills (In-In-Out-Out) Use an agility ladder. Step into each square with both feet, then step out. Focus on footwork and rhythm.
3. Shuttle Runs (10-20m) Sprint from a starting line to a cone, touch the ground, and sprint back to the start.
4. Side Shuffles Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Take quick side steps, keeping your chest up and back straight.
5. Forward-Backward Runs Run forward for a short distance, then quickly reverse and run backward.
6. Quick Feet Stand in place and quickly lift and lower your feet, barely leaving the ground.
7. Box Jumps (Low) Jump onto a low, stable box, focusing on a soft landing. Step back down.
8. A-Skips Skip forward, lifting your knees high while swinging your opposite arm.
9. Carioca A side-stepping movement where you cross one foot in front of the other, then behind.
10. Quick Box Steps Use a small box or step. Quickly step on and off, alternating feet.

For Intermediate

Exercise Description
1. T-Drill Run forward to a cone, shuffle right to a cone, shuffle left past the center cone, and then shuffle back to the center and run backward to the start.
2. L-Drill Sprint forward, cut and run sideways, cut and run backward, then cut and run sideways to the start.
3. Agility Cone Weave (Fast) Perform the cone weave with more speed and closer cone spacing.
4. Lateral Bounds Jump from side to side explosively, landing softly on one leg.
5. Box Jumps (Higher) Jump onto a higher box, focusing on explosive power and a soft landing.
6. Jump Rope (High Knees) Jump rope while bringing your knees up high with each jump.
7. Drop Jumps Step off a small box and immediately jump as high as you can when you hit the ground.
8. Single-Leg Agility Ladder Perform ladder drills on a single leg for increased balance and stability.
9. Broad Jumps Jump forward as far as you can, landing with bent knees.
10. Diagonal Runs Sprint forward, then quickly change direction at a 45-degree angle.

For Advanced

Exercise Description
1. Pro Agility Drill (5-10-5) Sprint 5 yards left, sprint 10 yards right, and sprint 5 yards left. Focus on quick changes of direction.
2. Ickey Shuffle A rapid ladder drill where your feet follow a specific pattern: in, out, in, out, in, in.
3. Box Jump Burpees Perform a burpee, and at the end of the movement, jump explosively onto a box.
4. Lateral Bound to Sprint Perform a lateral bound, land on one leg, and immediately sprint forward.
5. Medicine Ball Slams Lift a medicine ball overhead and slam it down with explosive force, using your entire body.
6. Flying 30s Sprint 30 meters from a running start, focusing on maximum speed.
7. Depth Jumps Step off a high box, and upon landing, immediately jump onto a second, higher box.
8. Power Skips for Distance Skip forward, focusing on maximum height and distance with each skip.
9. Single-Leg Lateral Jumps Jump from side to side on one leg, covering as much distance as possible.
10. Reactive Agility Drill A drill where a partner points in a direction and you immediately run in that direction.
