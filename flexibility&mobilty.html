Flexibility & Mobility Exercises

A comprehensive guide to improving flexibility and mobility for all fitness levels.

For Beginners

Exercise Description
1. Cat-Cow Stretch Come onto your hands and knees. Inhale, dropping your back and lifting your head (cow pose), and exhale, rounding your back and tucking your head (cat pose). This is excellent for spinal flexibility.
2. Child's Pose Sit on your knees, bend forward, and extend your arms forward. This stretches the lower back, hips, and shoulders.
3. Hamstring Stretch (Standing) Stand straight and place one leg forward with the heel on the ground and toes pointing up. Bend forward from your hips until you feel a stretch in your hamstring.
4. Seated Torso Twist Sit on the floor with your legs extended. Bend one knee and cross it over your other leg. Place the opposite arm over the bent knee and twist your back.
5. Knee-to-Chest Stretch Lie on your back on the floor and pull one knee towards your chest. Keep the other leg straight.
6. Glute Bridge Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Lift your hips up while keeping your core tight. This improves hip mobility.
7. Figure-Four Stretch Lie on your back. Bend one knee and place the ankle on the opposite thigh to form a "figure four". Lift the bottom leg and pull the hamstring towards you.
8. Wall Calf Stretch Use a wall for support. Place one leg back and keep your heel on the ground to feel a stretch in your calf.
9. Shoulder Rolls Roll your shoulders forward and then backward. This is good for shoulder joint mobility.
10. Neck Stretches Tilt your head to one side, trying to bring your ear toward your shoulder. Then tilt your head forward.

For Intermediate

Exercise Description
1. Downward Dog to Cobra This is a yoga flow where you transition from Downward Dog pose to Cobra pose. It stretches and mobilizes the entire body.
2. Pigeon Pose A deep hip stretch pose from yoga where one knee is bent forward and the other leg remains straight behind.
3. Spiderman Stretch Get into a lunge position and place both hands on the floor inside your front foot. This is very effective for hip and groin mobility.
4. Dynamic Lunges Lunge forward while swinging your arms to the side. This improves both the flexibility and strength of your hips and thighs.
5. Foam Rolling Use a foam roller to massage the tight muscles in your body. This helps to increase mobility and reduce muscle soreness.
6. Thoracic Spine Mobility Lie on your side on the floor with your knees bent up to your chest. Open your top arm while twisting your upper body.
7. World's Greatest Stretch Get into a lunge position and place your hands on the floor next to your front foot. Try to bring your front elbow to the floor and then raise your arm up.
8. Squat-to-Stand Place your feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart with toes pointing slightly outwards. Perform a full squat and then straighten your knees to lift your hips up.
9. Deep Squat Holds Hold a full squat position for a while without any support. This is very beneficial for hip and ankle mobility.
10. Ankle Rotations While lying or sitting, rotate your ankle in a circular motion in both directions.

For Advanced

Exercise Description
1. Couch Stretch Place one leg against a wall behind you with your knee on the floor to feel a deep stretch in your quadriceps.
2. Straddle Stretch Sit on the floor and spread your legs as wide as possible. Try to bend forward.
3. Frog Pose Get on your knees and hands, and spread your knees as wide as possible. This is a powerful stretch for the hip adductors.
4. Pancake Stretch Similar to the straddle stretch, but instead of bending straight forward, you try to bring your torso flat to the floor like a pancake.
5. Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch Place one knee on the floor and the other bent forward. Push your hips forward until you feel a stretch in the hip flexor of the back leg.
6. Full Backbend (Wheel Pose) Lie on your back on the floor and lift your body up on your hands and feet.
7. Pike Stretch (Seated) Sit on the floor with your legs straight in front of you. Try to grab your toes with your hands.
8. Shoulder Dislocates Hold a stick or rope and rotate it over your head and behind you, keeping your arms straight.
9. Lying Psoas Stretch Lie on the floor, keep one leg straight and pull the other bent leg towards your chest.
10. Wrist Rotations Make a fist with your hands and rotate your wrists in both directions to improve wrist joint mobility.
