Strength Training
Exercises

A comprehensive guide to building strength for all fitness levels.

For Beginners

Exercise Description
1. Goblet Squats Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell vertically against your chest. Stand with feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, toes pointing out. Lower your hips as if sitting in a chair, keeping your chest up and back straight. Return to the starting position.
2. Dumbbell Rows Hinge at your hips and slightly bend your knees. Place one hand on a bench for support. Hold a dumbbell in the other hand. Pull the dumbbell up towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blade. Lower the weight with control.
3. Push-Ups (on knees or against a wall) For a modification, start on your knees or with your hands on a wall. Keep your body in a straight line from your shoulders to your knees or heels. Lower your chest toward the floor or wall by bending your elbows, then push back up.
4. Bodyweight Lunges Stand with feet hip-width apart. Step forward with one leg and lower your hips until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Push off the front foot to return to the starting position.
5. Glute Bridges Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Drive through your heels to lift your hips off the floor until your body forms a straight line from your shoulders to your knees. Squeeze your glutes at the top, then lower back down.
6. Planks Get into a push-up position, but rest on your forearms with your elbows directly under your shoulders. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core. Hold this position.
7. Overhead Press (with light dumbbells) Stand or sit with your feet shoulder-width apart. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder level, palms facing forward. Press the weights straight up overhead until your arms are fully extended. Lower the weights with control.
8. Bicep Curls Stand with your feet hip-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing forward. Keep your elbows tucked at your sides and curl the weights up toward your shoulders. Lower the weights slowly.
9. Tricep Dips (on a bench) Sit on the edge of a bench and place your hands on the edge with your fingers pointing forward. Slide your hips off the bench and lower your body by bending your elbows. Push back up to the starting position.
10. Leg Raises Lie on your back with your legs straight. Keep your lower back pressed into the floor as you slowly raise your legs toward the ceiling. Lower them back down without letting them touch the floor.

For Intermediate

Exercise Description
1. Barbell Squats Place a barbell on your upper back. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your hips down and back as if sitting in a chair, keeping your chest up and back straight. Drive through your heels to stand back up.
2. Deadlifts Stand with your feet hip-width apart and the barbell in front of you. Bend at your hips and knees to grasp the bar with an overhand grip. Keep your back straight and lift the bar by straightening your hips and knees. Return the bar to the floor in a controlled manner.
3. Pull-Ups (assisted if needed) Use an assisted machine or a resistance band to help. Grasp a pull-up bar with an overhand grip. Pull your body up until your chin is over the bar. Lower with control.
4. Bench Press Lie on a flat bench with your feet on the floor. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower the bar to your chest, then press it back up.
5. Bent-Over Rows Hold a barbell with an overhand grip. Hinge at your hips, keeping your back straight and knees slightly bent. Pull the barbell up toward your stomach, squeezing your shoulder blades. Lower the bar with control.
6. Military Press Stand with a barbell at your chest, hands shoulder-width apart. Press the barbell straight up overhead until your arms are fully extended. Lower the weight slowly.
7. Dumbbell Lunges Hold a dumbbell in each hand. Take a step forward and lower your hips until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Push off your front foot to return to the starting position.
8. Box Jumps Stand in front of a sturdy box. Bend your knees and hips, then jump up and land softly on the box. Step back down.
9. Cable Rows Sit at a cable row machine with your feet on the footrests. Grasp the handle and pull it toward your abdomen, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Release the handle slowly.
10. Hanging Leg Raises Hang from a pull-up bar with an overhand grip. Keeping your legs straight, raise them up toward your chest. Lower them back down with control.

For Advanced

Exercise Description
1. Weighted Pull-Ups Perform a pull-up while wearing a weight belt or holding a dumbbell between your feet.
2. Weighted Dips Perform a dip while wearing a weight belt or holding a dumbbell between your feet.
3. Front Squats Place the barbell across the front of your shoulders, holding it with your hands. Perform a squat while keeping your chest upright.
4. Romanian Deadlifts (RDLs) Stand with a barbell in front of you. Hinge at your hips, keeping your legs mostly straight and your back flat, until the bar is below your knees. Return to the starting position.
5. Power Cleans A dynamic, full-body lift. Start with the barbell on the floor, and in one explosive movement, lift it to your shoulders, ending in a partial squat.
6. Snatch A highly technical, explosive lift where you lift the barbell from the floor to overhead in one continuous motion.
7. One-Arm Dumbbell Snatch Perform the snatch movement using a single dumbbell.
8. Handstand Push-Ups Against a wall, get into a handstand position. Lower your head toward the floor by bending your elbows, then press back up.
9. Pistol Squats Perform a squat on one leg, extending the other leg straight out in front of you.
10. Muscle-Ups A combination of a pull-up and a dip. Hang from a bar, pull yourself up, and transition into a dip, pushing your body up and over the bar.
