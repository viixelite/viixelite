Functional Training
Exercises

Improve real-life strength, mobility, and power with functional movements.

For Beginners

Exercise Description
1. Bodyweight SquatStand with feet shoulder-width apart. Push hips back and bend knees as if sitting in a chair. Keep chest up and core tight, then return to standing.
2. Hip HingeStand tall, hands behind your head. Push hips back, lean forward with a flat back, then return upright.
3. Glute BridgeLie on your back with knees bent. Push through heels to lift hips, squeeze glutes at the top, and lower back down.
4. Step-Ups (Low Platform)Step onto a low bench with one foot, bring the other up, then step down. Repeat for both legs.
5. Wall Push-UpsStand facing a wall, place palms flat, bend elbows to bring chest toward wall, then push back to start.
6. Carry Walk (Light Weights)Hold light weights at your sides and walk slowly, keeping shoulders back and core engaged.
7. Bird DogOn hands and knees, extend right arm and left leg. Hold 2–3 sec, then switch sides.
8. Standing Calf RaiseStand tall, lift heels to rise on toes, pause briefly, then lower slowly.
9. Reverse LungeStep one foot backward, lower into lunge, push through front foot to stand. Switch legs.
10. Side Step with BandPlace resistance band above knees. Bend knees slightly and step sideways, keeping band tight.

For Intermediate

Exercise Description
1. Kettlebell DeadliftStand with feet hip-width apart, hold kettlebell. Push hips back, bend slightly at knees, lower weight, then stand tall again.
2. Dumbbell Overhead PressHold dumbbells at shoulders. Press overhead until arms are straight, then lower slowly.
3. Push-UpsStart in plank, lower chest toward ground, then push back up while keeping body straight.
4. Walking LungesStep forward into lunge, push through heel, and bring back leg forward to continue walking.
5. Medicine Ball SlamHold ball overhead with straight arms, slam down to ground forcefully, pick up and repeat.
6. Plank with Shoulder TapHold plank, tap opposite shoulder with one hand while keeping hips steady.
7. Single-Leg Romanian DeadliftBalance on one leg, hinge at hips, extend opposite leg back, then return upright. Switch legs.
8. Farmer Carry (Heavier Weights)Hold heavy dumbbells/kettlebells at sides, walk 20–40 steps with shoulders back and core braced.
9. Box Step-Ups (Higher Box)Step onto a higher bench/box, drive through heel, stand tall, then step down carefully.
10. Russian TwistsSit on floor, lean back slightly, hold weight, twist torso side to side, touching floor each time.

For Advanced

Exercise Description
1. Kettlebell SwingStand with feet wide, swing kettlebell between legs with a hip hinge, drive hips forward to chest height.
2. Clean & PressLift weight from floor to shoulder (clean), then press overhead until arms lock out. Lower with control.
3. Burpee with JumpFrom standing, drop into plank, do push-up, jump feet forward, and explode up into a jump.
4. Pull-Ups (Weighted if possible)Grip bar overhead, pull chin above bar, then lower slowly under control.
5. Medicine Ball Rotational ThrowStand sideways to wall, hold ball, rotate torso, and throw ball explosively into wall.
6. Single-Leg Box JumpsStand on one leg in front of box, jump explosively onto box, land softly on same leg.
7. Turkish Get-UpLie holding kettlebell overhead, rise step-by-step to standing while keeping arm straight, then return down.
8. Sled Push (or Heavy Push)Lean forward and push sled or heavy object, driving with powerful strides.
9. Battle RopesGrip ropes, perform alternating waves or explosive slams for 20–30 seconds.
10. Overhead Walking LungeHold weight overhead with arms locked, perform walking lunges while keeping core tight.
