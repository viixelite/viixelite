Endurance Training
Exercises

A complete guide to building stamina, cardiovascular fitness, and muscular endurance.

For Beginners

Exercise Description
1. Brisk WalkingWalk at a steady, fast pace. Keep arms swinging naturally. Continue for 15–20 minutes.
2. Jogging in PlaceJog without moving forward. Lift knees slightly and pump arms. Continue for 30–60 seconds.
3. Jumping JacksJump while spreading feet and raising arms overhead. Return to start and repeat for 20–30 reps.
4. Bodyweight SquatsStand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower hips down and back, then return to standing. Perform 12–15 reps.
5. Stationary LungesStep one foot forward, bend both knees to 90°. Push back to starting position. Do 10 reps per leg.
6. Step-Ups (Low Step)Step onto a low bench with one foot, bring the other up, then step down. Repeat 10 reps per leg.
7. Plank HoldRest on forearms, elbows under shoulders. Keep body straight from head to heels. Hold for 20–30 seconds.
8. High Knees (Light Pace)Jog in place, lifting knees toward chest at a steady rhythm. Continue for 20–30 seconds.
9. Cycling (Easy Pace)Ride a stationary or outdoor bike at a comfortable speed. Continue for 10–15 minutes.
10. Rope Skipping (Light)Jump lightly with a rope at a steady rhythm. Perform 20–30 skips.

For Intermediate

Exercise Description
1. Running/Jog IntervalsAlternate between 1 min jogging and 30 sec running. Repeat for 10–15 minutes.
2. BurpeesFrom standing, squat down, jump to plank, do a push-up, jump forward, and explode up. Repeat 8–12 reps.
3. Mountain ClimbersStart in plank. Drive knees toward chest alternately at a fast pace. Continue for 20–30 seconds.
4. Jump Rope (Continuous)Jump rope steadily for 1–2 minutes. Keep jumps light and controlled.
5. Push-Ups (High Reps)Perform push-ups with good form. Aim for 12–15 continuous reps.
6. Air Squat JumpsDo a squat, then jump explosively upward. Land softly and repeat 12–15 times.
7. Cycling (Moderate Pace)Ride steadily at a challenging but manageable pace for 20–30 minutes.
8. Rowing MachineRow smoothly at a steady rhythm for 8–10 minutes. Focus on full strokes.
9. Swimming (Continuous Laps)Swim at an easy pace without stopping. Continue for 5–10 minutes.
10. Circuit TrainingChoose 3–4 bodyweight moves (e.g., squats, push-ups, jumping jacks). Perform back-to-back, 2–3 rounds.

For Advanced

Exercise Description
1. Long-Distance RunningRun at a steady pace for 30–60 minutes. Maintain consistent breathing and form.
2. HIIT SprintsSprint for 30 sec, then walk/jog for 1 min. Repeat for 8–10 rounds.
3. Jump Rope Double UndersSpin the rope twice under your feet per jump. Continue for 30–60 seconds.
4. Burpee to Pull-UpPerform a burpee, then jump up to a bar and complete a pull-up. Repeat 8–12 reps.
5. Kettlebell SwingsHinge at hips, swing kettlebell between legs, then forward to chest height. Perform 15–20 reps.
6. Box JumpsJump explosively onto a sturdy box. Land softly and step down. Repeat 12–15 times.
7. Cycling (Fast Pace)Ride at a high speed continuously for 45–60 minutes. Maintain steady breathing.
8. Rowing Machine SprintsRow 250m as fast as possible. Rest 1 min. Repeat 6 times.
9. Swimming IntervalsSwim one fast lap, rest briefly, then repeat. Complete 8 total laps.
10. CrossFit-Style AMRAPSelect 4 moves (squats, push-ups, burpees, climbers). Perform as many rounds as possible in 10–15 minutes.
